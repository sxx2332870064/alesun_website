About Me

"WHY HUMANS?"

Bridging Humanity and Digital Realms

I'm a digital artist passionate about merging humanity with digital media. My portfolio features digital paintings, illustrations, animations, video editing, and coding projects involving software operations and hardware installations. I explore the interplay between organic bodies and the limitless potential of digital art, aiming to provoke thought and inspire contemplation within the virtual realm.

Exploring Futures and Multiverses

My art explores apocalyptic scenarios, science-fiction innovations, human-robot dynamics, and humanized animals to envision possible futures given current social issues. I aim to provoke reflection and dialogue about the world we're creating and the paths we're on. Welcome to my vision of tomorrow.

Unveiling the Essence of AI

I delve into the world of AI-generated content and the construction of AI setups. My art brings AI to life through robotic installations and animated stories. Join me in exploring the fusion of technology and creativity in the evolving human-AI relationship.

ANIMATION

Watch out (2020)
"Watch out" is my debut animation, a hand-drawn, frame-by-frame GIF created in 2020, capturing the essence of motion and expression in a simple yet impactful way.

cyb-heartbeat (2022)
"cyb-heartbeat" is a hand-drawn, frame-by-frame animation created in 2022. Set against a backdrop of a doll I crafted, this GIF brings to life the intersection of art and technology, exploring themes of artificial emotion.

Phone-nomena (2023)
"Phone-nomena" (2023) is an animation exploring the dangers of over-reliance on phones and electronic devices. It portrays a breaking point where dependence on technology becomes harmful, using striking visuals and sound to create a powerful impact.

hello_world dreams (2022)
"hello_world dreams" (2022) tells the story of an artificial intelligence, critiquing human manipulation and overuse of AI. The animation uses a female figure to highlight gender discrimination and inequalities, emphasizing the complex relationship between technology and societal issues.

Recoil (2023)
"Recoil" (2023) is a mixed-media video blending 2D and 3D animations with real-life footage to create a unique visual experience. It explores themes of nature protection, highlighting the destructive impact of human technological overdevelopment and environmental exploitation, ultimately envisioning an apocalyptic end. The half-human, half-animal figure symbolizes our intrinsic connection to nature.

Chronicles of the Lamb (2023)
"Chronicles of the Lamb" (2023) explores how biases, myths, and stereotypes fuel misunderstandings and discrimination. Featuring a half-human, half-animal figure, this animation challenges typical perceptions of lambs, urging viewers to recognize how everyone can be misunderstood and encouraging a broader, unbiased perspective.

What to Eat? (2023)
"What to Eat?" was created in 2023 as a 3D animation entirely made in Quill on a VR headset, encompassing modeling, rigging, keyframing, animating, and rendering. This hand-drawn-like 3D style provides a unique visual impact, allowing audiences to view it from any angle in VR. The story humorously depicts a robot using a battery as bait while fishing, highlighting the importance of thinking beyond personal biases and assumptions.

MEOW! (2023)
"MEOW!" (2023) is a fully 3D animation depicting a playful cat outsmarting house maintenance AI robots, only to reveal itself as a robot too. This twist highlights how appearances can deceive, suggesting that technology can mislead humans just as easily as it serves them.

The Shadow (2024)
"The Shadow" (2024) blends 3D and 2D animation to create a visually striking narrative. It follows a future human, trapped emotionally in their room, and their animated shadow, which helps them reconnect with others. This piece explores self-healing and the dual impact of overdeveloped technology on human connections.

ROBOTIC

Arcane Orb (2023)
"Arcane Orb" (2023) combines Arduino Nano, a tilt sensor, LED light strips, and 3D modeling to create a magical ball that responds to directional movements with preset color sequences. This artwork explores the interplay between human physical actions, robotics, and emotional values, highlighting their interconnectedness.

Lights Out (2023)
"Lights Out" was created in 2023 using Arduino Nano, flex sensors, LED light strips (Neopixels), LED light lamps in a ring, ion battery, 3D modeling, and 3D printing, along with various fabrications. This wearable robotic glove shuts off lights when the user makes a fist and illuminates when they stretch their hand, exploring robotics integration in daily life and introducing cyborg concepts.

REDS! (2023)
"REDS!" was created in 2023 using MAX8 software, Arduino Uno, a USB camera, an LCD screen, a step motor, and 3D modeling and printing. This artwork detects live video, processing it in MAX to identify the color red. The robot complains if no red is detected and turns its head towards any red it finds, mimicking human perception and reactions. It questions the differences between robotics and humans, exploring the boundaries of technology.

CURRENT PROJECT: AI-Driven Interactive Exhibit
My current project integrates AI, moving image, and robotics to create an immersive, futuristic experience. The setup involves a dynamic HTML website and AI systems that analyze audio and video inputs, generate interactive dialogues, and create personalized character profiles and stories in a post-apocalyptic multiverse setting. This project explores AI's potential in storytelling and human-robot interaction.
